# AI in Fashion Retail – Business Strategy & Ethical Analysis

Course: Artificial Intelligence in Business  
University of Canterbury – 2024  

## Overview

This project evaluates the strategic implementation of AI-powered “Body Scan Jeans” technology used by H&M and proposes its integration for Hallenstein Glassons (NZ).

The project analyzes:

- AI strategy alignment
- Implementation roadmap (POC → Pilot → Roll-out)
- Cost-benefit evaluation
- Technical & infrastructure requirements
- Ethical risks and bias considerations
- Māori cultural and data sovereignty implications

---

## Part 1: Industry Use Case

Analyzed H&M’s AI-powered body scanning technology:

- 3D body scanning & machine learning modeling
- On-demand manufacturing for sustainability
- Customer satisfaction metrics
- Operational efficiency impact

---

## Part 2: Business Case Proposal

Proposed implementation plan for Hallenstein Glassons:

- Technical infrastructure requirements
- Risk mitigation strategy
- Stakeholder involvement
- Training & change management plan
- Evaluation & scalability framework

---

## Ethical & Cultural Analysis

Additional research focused on:

- Algorithmic bias risks
- Data privacy concerns
- Regulatory compliance
- Māori data sovereignty and inclusivity considerations
